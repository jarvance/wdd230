Hick's Law

Craigslist

Website link

Hick's law states that the more stimuli, or choices on a page, the longer users take to make decisions. Too many choices can cause people to be indecisive, when they normally would have. This can lead to loss of sales and revenue. Criagslist does a good job of categorizing everything in an easy to read and direct manner.

White Space

Google

Website link

White space is unmarked space between elements, texts, etc. However, the space doesn't literally have to be white; colored backgrounds and patterns can still elevate the rest of the page. Google uses this concept very well. When making a search, the page is very clean and it's easy for the user to focus exactly on what he/she wants.

Rule of Thirds

Amazon

Website link

Pages and images split into thirds are naturally more appealing. Amazon splits all of its products into grids of three.
